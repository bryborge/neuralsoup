# Row Addition

## Core Concept

> Row addition is one of the [[202603062350-elementary-row-operations|elementary row operations]] that can be executed on a matrix. The operation is as follows:
>
>   1. multiply the entries of a given row ($R_1$)
>   2. add each entry in $R_1$ to each corresponding entry in another row ($R_2$), and
>   3. replace $R_2$ with the result

## Explanation

The purpose of this operation is to create a zero entry ($0$) below the pivot and aid in the pursuit of finding the [[202603050054-row-echelon-form|echelon form]] of the matrix.

## Example

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}
1 & 2 & 5\\
3 & -1 & 4
\end{array}\right]
\xrightarrow{-3(R_1) + R_2, \rightarrow \,R_2}
\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}
1 & 2 & 5\\
0 & -7 & -11
\end{array}\right]
\end{aligned}
$$
