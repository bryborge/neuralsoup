# Row Swap

## Core Concept

> Row swapping is one of the [[202603062350-elementary-row-operations|elementary row operations]] that can be executed on a matrix. The operation is simply to switch any two rows in a matrix with one another.

## Explanation

The purpose of this operation is to create a zero entry ($0$) below the pivot (or make it simpler to do so in a follow up operation) and aid in the pursuit of finding the [[202603050054-row-echelon-form|echelon form]] of the matrix.

## Example

$$
\begin{aligned}
&\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}
1 & 2 & 5\\
3 & -1 & 4
\end{array}\right]
\xrightarrow{\,R_1\leftrightarrow R_2\,}
\left[\begin{array}{cc|c}
3 & -1 & 4\\
1 & 2 & 5
\end{array}\right]
\end{aligned}
$$
