# Pi

## Core Concept

> Pi ($\pi$) is a [[Universal Constant]] that represents the [[Fixed Ratio]] of a circle's [[Circumference]] and its [[Diameter]].

$$
\pi = \frac{C}{d}
$$
*(where '$C$' is the circumference and '$d$' is the diameter of a given circle)*

## Explanation

As a pure number, $\pi \approx 3.14159$.

It _emerges_ naturally from a [[Plane]] when a fixed point ([[Invariant Point]]) and a fixed distance is introduced. While $\pi$ is not part of the literal definition of a plane, it can be argued that $\pi$ is a "latent" entity in a plane, waiting for the moment that the plane is used to measure something inside of it.

## Example

**Given:** A pizza measuring 12 inches in diameter.

**Find:** Its area in inches ($in^2$).

**Solution:**

1.  Find the radius ($r$).
    $$
    r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{12}{2} = 6
    $$

2.  Use the radius to find area ($A$).
    $$
    A = \pi r^2 = \pi \cdot (6)^2 \approx 113 \space in^2
    $$
