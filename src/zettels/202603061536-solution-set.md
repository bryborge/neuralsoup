# Solution Set

## Core Concept

> Solution sets are exactly what the name implies -- the set of all solutions for a [[202603052321-system-of-linear-equations|system of linear equations]].

## Explanation

If two [[202603052321-system-of-linear-equations|linear systems]] have the same solution set, the are said to be [[202603062330-row-equivalent-matrices|row equivalent]].

## Example

**Given:** The following linear system.
$$
\begin{align*}
2x_1 - 3x_2 &= 1 \\
6x_1 - 8x_2 &= 5
\end{align*}
$$

**Find:** The solution set for the linear system.

**Solution:**

1.  We solve the system using elementary row operations:

    $$
    \begin{bmatrix}
    2 & -3 & | & 1 \\
    6 & -8 & | & 5
    \end{bmatrix}
    \xrightarrow{R_1 \div 2}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & -\frac{3}{2} & | & \frac{1}{2} \\
    6 & -8 & | & 5
    \end{bmatrix}
    \xrightarrow{R_2 - 6R_1}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & -\frac{3}{2} & | & \frac{1}{2} \\
    0 & 1 & | & 2
    \end{bmatrix}
    \xrightarrow{R_1 + \frac{3}{2}R_2}
    \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & | & \frac{7}{2} \\
    0 & 1 & | & 2
    \end{bmatrix}
    $$

2.  We can now express these findings in the original terms:

    $$
    \begin{cases}
      x_1 = \frac{7}{2} \\
      x_2 = 2
    \end{cases}
    $$

3.  Final solution set:

    $$
    \boxed{\left\{\left(\frac{7}{2},\ 2\right)\right\}}
    $$
