## Key Terms & Definitions

- **Dot product (inner product):** The sum of the products of corresponding entries of two vectors; measures similarity.
- **Norm (length):** The non-negative scalar representing the length of a vector, denoted ||v||.
- **Unit vector:** A vector of length 1.
- **Orthogonal vectors:** Vectors whose dot product is zero.
- **Orthogonal set:** A set of vectors where each pair is orthogonal.
- **Orthonormal set:** An orthogonal set of unit vectors.
- **Orthogonal projection:** The projection of a vector onto another vector or subspace, minimizing distance.
- **Best Approximation Theorem:** States that the orthogonal projection of a vector onto a subspace is the closest point in that subspace to the vector.
- **Transition matrix:** A matrix that converts coordinates from one basis to another (if mentioned in this lecture).
