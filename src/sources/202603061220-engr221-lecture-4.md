## Key Terms & Definitions

- **Linear Circuit:** A circuit composed of independent sources and linear elements.
- **Linear Element:** A passive device with a linear current-voltage (i-v) relationship, such as a resistor, capacitor, or inductor.
- **Ohm's Law:** The relationship v = iR between voltage, current, and resistance.
- **Independent Source:** A source whose value is not dependent on any other element in the circuit.
- **Dependent Source:** A source whose value depends on another variable in the circuit.
- **KCL (Kirchhoff's Current Law):** The sum of currents entering a node equals the sum leaving it.
- **KVL (Kirchhoff's Voltage Law):** The sum of voltages around any closed loop is zero.
- **Simultaneous Equations:** Equations required to solve for unknown currents or voltages in a circuit.
- **Series Circuit:** Components connected end-to-end, sharing the same current.
- **Parallel Circuit:** Components connected across the same two nodes, sharing the same voltage.
- **Wye-Delta Transformation:** A method to convert between wye (Y) and delta (Δ) resistor networks.
- **Wheatstone Bridge:** A circuit used to measure unknown resistances.
- **DMM (Digital Multimeter):** An instrument for measuring voltage, current, and resistance.
- **Req (Equivalent Resistance):** The total resistance of a series, parallel, or series-parallel circuit.
