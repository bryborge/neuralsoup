# Source: MTH261 - Lecture 2

## tl;dr

> Introduction to echelon and reduced echelon forms, the row reduction algorithm, and their use in solving and analyzing linear systems.

## Key Topics

- Echelon form and reduced echelon form
- Row reduction algorithm (Gaussian elimination)
- Pivot positions, pivot columns, and pivots
- General and particular solutions of linear systems
- Consistency criteria for linear systems
- Applications: temperature grids, polynomial interpolation, and modeling with linear systems

## Key Terms & Definitions

- **Echelon form:** A matrix form with all nonzero rows above any rows of all zeros, leading entries to the right, and zeros below leading entries.
- **Reduced echelon form:** An echelon form where leading entries are 1 and are the only nonzero entries in their columns.
- **Row reduction algorithm (Gaussian elimination):** A step-by-step process to transform a matrix into echelon or reduced echelon form.
- **Pivot position:** The location of a leading entry in echelon form.
- **Pivot column:** A column containing a pivot position.
- **Pivot:** A nonzero number in a pivot position.
- **Basic variable:** A variable corresponding to a pivot column.
- **Free variable:** A variable not corresponding to a pivot column; can take any value.
- **General solution:** The set of all possible solutions to a linear system, expressed in terms of free variables.
- **Consistent system:** A linear system with at least one solution.
- **Augmented matrix:** A matrix representing a linear system, including the coefficients and constants.
