## Key Terms & Definitions

- **Conductivity (σ):** Measure of how easily electrons drift through a material under voltage.
- **Resistivity (ρ):** Inverse of conductivity; measures how much a material resists electron flow.
- **Resistance (R):** The ability of a material to oppose electron flow; measured in Ohms (Ω).
- **Resistor:** A device used to oppose electron flow in a circuit.
- **Ohm's Law:** States that voltage (v) across a conductor is proportional to current (i) through it; v = iR.
- **Conductance (G):** The reciprocal of resistance; measured in Siemens (S).
- **Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL):** The sum of currents entering a node equals the sum leaving it.
- **Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL):** The sum of voltages around any closed loop is zero.
- **Equivalent Resistance (Req):** The total resistance of a series or parallel network.
- **Voltage Divider:** A circuit that divides input voltage among series resistors proportionally.
- **Current Divider:** A parallel circuit that divides input current among branches.
- **Source Transformation:** Converting between equivalent voltage-source and current-source circuits.
