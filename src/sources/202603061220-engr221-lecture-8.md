## Key Terms & Definitions

- **Capacitor:** A passive electrical component that stores electrical charge and has the property of capacitance.
- **Capacitance (C):** The ability of a capacitor to store charge, measured in Farads (F).
- **Dielectric:** The insulating material between the plates of a capacitor.
- **Inductor:** A passive electrical component formed by a coil of wire, which exhibits the property of inductance.
- **Inductance (L):** A measure of a coil’s ability to establish an induced voltage as a result of a change in its current, measured in Henrys (H).
- **Transient Response:** The temporary behavior of a circuit immediately after a change in its conditions.
- **Steady-State Response:** The behavior of a circuit after it has settled from any transient effects.
- **Nonperiodic Waveform:** A signal that does not repeat at regular intervals (e.g., step, ramp, pulse, exponential).
