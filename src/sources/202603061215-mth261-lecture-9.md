## Key Terms & Definitions

- **Determinant (det A):** A scalar value associated with a square matrix, indicating invertibility and linear independence.
- **Cofactor (Cᵢⱼ):** (−1)^(i+j) times the determinant of the submatrix obtained by removing row i and column j.
- **Cofactor expansion:** Method for computing determinants by expanding along a row or column.
- **Triangular matrix:** A square matrix with all entries either above (upper) or below (lower) the main diagonal as zero.
- **Main diagonal:** Entries a₁₁, a₂₂, ..., aₙₙ in a square matrix.
- **Invertible matrix:** A matrix with a nonzero determinant and an inverse.
- **Singular matrix:** A matrix with determinant zero; not invertible.
- **Row reduction:** Process of using row operations to simplify a matrix, often to echelon form.
- **Linearly independent:** A set of vectors whose matrix has a nonzero determinant.
- **Transpose (Aᵗ):** The matrix obtained by switching rows and columns; det(A) = det(Aᵗ).
- **Product property:** det(AB) = det(A)det(B) for square matrices A and B.
